My Academic Experience

I owe a great many thanks to Prof David Gleich and his graduate students at Purdue University, who have encouraged my interest in research and who have been patiently helping me overcome my lack of experience and direction. I worked on a project with D. Gleich and Nate Veldt involving parallelizing some Graph Clustering techniques. We have written a paper over this work and are currently working to submit it to different conferences.

Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1901.10084
Code: https://github.com/camruggles/ParallelDykstras

Short Summary: In metric constrained optimization, it is possible to update multiple variables at a time without violating constraints or correctness. I found a method that can handle O(n) variable updates at the same time, out of O(n^3) variable updates in total.

Before this I had done some work on simulating second order markov chains on large social networks, and trying to find ways to figure out network separation of a mixed Erdos Renyi and Barabasi Albert graph.

Internship

During the Summer of 2019, I worked at a company called Ciholas. They are in the space of Ultra Wideband Networks. My job was to implement and tweak filtering algorithms in Python to enhance sensor position data. This involved quickly getting up to speed on multivariate gaussian distributions. The resulting filters demonstrated better latency compared to previous, less sophisticated filters and significant improvement in location accuracy over raw data.
