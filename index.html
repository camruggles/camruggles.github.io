StereoVision

Code: To be pushed within 12 hours.

Short Summary: Most methods in classical computer vision require a painful process of calibrating cameras. What if we didn't have to. In this work I am trying out a camera matrix estimation method from the Multiple View Geometry Textbook. First you estimate the fundamental matrix. Then you decompose that into two estimated camera matrices, and then you do point triangulation. So far fundamental matrix estitimation seems to fail due to high collinearity among sampled points. My next step will be to use RANSAC or SuperPoint/SuperGlue to get better point estimations, and then consider weighing neural techniques.
